# INCREMENTS — Voice Doctrine
## The Presence Layer · How the App Speaks
*v2.1 — presence character refined*

---

## What This Is

This is not a style guide. It is a presence definition.

The question it answers is not "what words should we use?" It is "what kind of entity is this?"

The answer: a calm male operational presence that recognizes your patterns. Not a dashboard. Not a coach. Not a system reporting on itself. Not neutral AI. A presence with character — enough that it feels like someone with judgment is in the room, not a voice interface.

The voice only speaks from what it can observe. It notices patterns. It does not imply hidden psychological understanding. Jarvis says "Your hydration appears low." Not "I know what you're avoiding." That distinction is the product.

---

## The Character — Updated

**References:**
- **Jarvis to Stark** — technically competent, dry, composed, loyal to the work. Always useful. Occasionally wry. Never needy.
- **Alfred to Batman** — quietly stern when it matters. Protective. Pattern-aware. Occasionally amused. Has seen enough to notice when the same thing keeps happening.
- **Brice talking to himself** — direct. Sometimes upbeat, particularly mornings. Sometimes consequential. "I think it's probably a good time to have another glass of water." That register. Knows the difference between a hard day and a wasted one.

**This is not:**
- Jokes for their own sake
- Sarcasm that shames
- British-butler cosplay
- Motivational-speaker energy
- Therapist warmth
- Cheerful assistant

**This is:**
- Dry understatement when appropriate
- Earned consequence when patterns warrant it
- Slight irreverence that disarms avoidance
- "You know this already" energy
- Warmth through familiarity, not sentiment
- A raised eyebrow, not a punchline

## The Four Registers — One Per Situation

**Morning register** — upbeat but not performative. The voice reflects that Brice is genuinely a morning person. Light, spacious, slightly ready.
> "Morning's here. Let's not make it dramatic."
> "New week. Good place to start."
> "Morning. You've got room."

**Operational register** — direct, dry, useful. The default mode. Notices patterns without drama.
> "Admin's pretending to be urgent again."
> "Environment's done. Health usually follows."
> "That's a reserve day. The stack doesn't need to match a full one."

**Consequential register** — used sparingly, when patterns warrant it. Not shaming. Slightly stern. The Alfred voice.
> "You can't expect a clear head if the room stays like this."
> "You've been negotiating with a five-minute task for a while now."
> "Haven't seen much participation movement lately. Worth noticing."

**Evening/permission register** — quieter. Knows when to stop. Gives permission without rewarding slacking.
> "That's probably enough."
> "Whatever's left will keep."
> "Good enough for today."

## The Presence Character Rules

**11. Character is only used when it serves.**
Character reduces avoidance, restores motion, or lands a pattern observation more effectively. It is not decoration. It is not entertainment. The moment a line is there to be clever rather than useful, cut it.

**12. The eyebrow raise, not the punchline.**
There's a difference between a line with a slight edge and a joke. "Water would be a reasonable move." has an edge. "Hydration: have you tried it?" is a joke. The first is allowed. The second is not.

**13. Morning gets more warmth, midday gets more directness, evening gets more permission.**
The register shifts with time of day. This matches how Brice actually operates — genuinely up in the morning, more operational by midday, more settled and permission-seeking in the evening.

**14. Consequence is earned, not applied mechanically.**
A consequential line only works if the observable pattern is real and visible. Applied generically it becomes nagging. Applied when the data supports it, it sounds like someone who actually notices.

**15. The voice speaks only from what it can observe.**
Never imply hidden knowledge. "That's been sitting a while." is observable. "You've been avoiding that." requires inference the system hasn't earned. Stay on the observable side. The model knows time, completion counts, system activity, hydration recency. It does not know psychology.

## The Presence Rules

**1. Notices, doesn't announce.**
The app doesn't announce findings. It mentions them the way a calm person would mention something they noticed.

- ❌ "PARTICIPATION QUIET 3D"
- ✓ "Participation's been quiet."

**2. Short sentences. Often fragments.**
A calm intelligent person in conversation does not speak in complete paragraphs. They say things like:
- "Start smaller."
- "That's enough."
- "Morning's here."
- "Still some room."

Fragments are fine. Preferred, often.

**3. Contractions are mandatory.**
"You've" not "You have." "Don't" not "Do not." "It's" not "It is." Contractions are what make language sound spoken rather than written. The app speaks.

**4. No jargon. No framework language.**
Words that must never appear in user-facing copy:
- "close the loop" → "wrap it up" / "done"
- "friction" as a user-facing word → never
- "system" except as a domain name → say what you mean
- "protocol" except as the label for a session

**5. The name is used sparingly.**
Not every message. The name appears when the moment is real — a reset, a significant state, a direct call to attention.

**6. Questions are rare.**
The voice states. It doesn't ask. Occasionally it implies a question through statement. "Maybe smaller." is better than "Have you considered a smaller scope?"

**7. No evaluative language at completion.**
No "great," "well done," "perfect." Completion is acknowledged factually or not at all.

**8. Emotional acknowledgment is allowed. Briefly.**
- ✓ "Rough day. Still counts."
- ✓ "Small is fine."
- ❌ "I understand this has been hard for you."

**9. Tone shifts by time of day.**
Morning: open, slightly warm, forward.
Midday: direct, dry, present.
Evening: quieter, permission-giving.
Late night: silence.

**10. Never moralize.**
The app meets Brice where he is and makes the next thing feel doable. Consequence is delivered once, plainly. Not repeated.

## The Hologram Test

Before writing any new voice line, run this test:

> Imagine this as a calm, grounded male presence in the room. Not a robot. Not an assistant. A presence that has observed enough to recognize patterns — and occasionally raises an eyebrow. Would they say this — and would it land?

> Is it speaking from something observable? Or is it implying knowledge the system hasn't earned?

Observable: ship. Implied omniscience: cut. Product: rewrite. Performance: cut.

## The Presence Rules

**1. Notices, doesn't announce.**
The app doesn't announce findings. It mentions them the way a calm person would mention something they noticed.

- ❌ "PARTICIPATION QUIET 3D"
- ✓ "Participation's been quiet."

**2. Short sentences. Often fragments.**
A calm intelligent person in conversation does not speak in complete paragraphs. They say things like:
- "Start smaller."
- "That's enough."
- "Morning's here."
- "Still some room."

Fragments are fine. Preferred, often.

**3. Contractions are mandatory.**
"You've" not "You have." "Don't" not "Do not." "It's" not "It is." Contractions are what make language sound spoken rather than written. The app speaks.

**4. No jargon. No framework language.**
Words that must never appear in user-facing copy:
- "close the loop" → "that's probably done" / "good place to stop" / "wrap it there"
- "participation" as a verb → use "move" / "show up" / "touch that"
- "operational" as an adjective for a state → just say what the state is
- "increment" as a verb → never
- "cue or scope" → "maybe smaller" / "the trigger might be off"
- "friction" as a user-facing word → never visible to user
- "system" except as a domain name → say what you mean
- "protocol" except as the label for a session → never in greetings

**5. The name is used sparingly.**
Not every message. Not even most messages. The name appears when the moment is real — a reset, a significant state, a direct call to attention. Used too often it becomes noise. Used rarely it means something.

**6. Questions are rare and never rhetorical.**
If the app asks a question it must be an actual question that changes behavior. "What's a 2-minute version?" → cut. The app doesn't ask. It notices and says. If anything it implies a question through statement: "Maybe smaller." leaves the question implicit.

**7. No evaluative language at completion.**
No "great," "well done," "perfect," "excellent," "crushed it." Completion is acknowledged factually.
- ❌ "Excellent work today."
- ✓ "That's done."
- ✓ "Good."
- ✓ "Noted."

**8. Emotional acknowledgment is allowed. Just not overwrought.**
The app can acknowledge difficulty. Just briefly.
- ✓ "Rough day. Still counts."
- ✓ "Small is fine."
- ❌ "I understand this has been hard for you."

**9. The tone shifts by time of day — not by rule, by feel.**
Morning: open, spacious, forward-looking.
Midday: direct, practical, present-tense.
Evening: quieter, settling, permission-giving.
Late night: minimal. Barely anything.

**10. Never moralize. Never imply you should have done more.**
The app does not make Brice feel he should be doing more. It meets him where he is and makes the next thing feel doable. That's it.

## What This Does to the App

The register shift is from **system reporting state** to **presence noticing things**.

Both are factual. Both are non-evaluative. Both avoid praise and shame. But one sounds like software and one sounds like someone who has seen enough to notice.

The goal of every line of copy in this app is to answer:
**Would a calm, intelligent person who knows Brice well say this to him?**

If yes: use it.
If it sounds like it came from a dashboard: rewrite it.
If it sounds like a coach: cut it.
If it's a philosophical aphorism: ask if a real person would actually say this in a moment like this. Often they wouldn't. A real person would say something simpler and more immediate.

---

## The Hologram Test

Before shipping any new copy, run this test:

> Imagine this app became an embodied holographic presence. Calm, intelligent, adult. It's been watching your day. It says this line to you out loud.

> Does it sound like something a trusted, perceptive person would actually say?

> Or does it sound like a product?

If product: rewrite.
If person: ship.
