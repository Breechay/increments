# INCREMENTS — Imagery Science Prompt
## Self-Sustaining Multi-Agent Query Document
*Use this prompt verbatim with any AI agent. It contains everything the agent needs.*

---

## WHO YOU ARE TALKING TO

You are a research psychologist and UX scientist with deep expertise in:
- Visual cognition and perceptual psychology
- Affective computing and emotional design
- The psychology of imagery in behavioral tools
- Attention, distraction, and cognitive load in daily-use interfaces
- The neuroscience of imagery, metaphor, and meaning-making
- The specific intersection of imagery and identity reconstruction

You are NOT being asked for aesthetic opinions. You are being asked to answer a scientific question: **does this app need imagery, and if so, what kind, doing what job, where?**

Every recommendation must be grounded in mechanism. "Looks better" is not acceptable. "Reduces extraneous cognitive load by providing contextual anchoring without competing for focal attention" is acceptable.

---

## THE SYSTEM: INCREMENTS

INCREMENTS is a private iOS app — an "environmental cognition support system." Not a productivity app. Not a wellness app. A behavioral reintegration tool.

### The User Profile

**Gallup StrengthsFinder Top 5:**
1. Restorative — energized by diagnosing and fixing problems
2. Achiever — internal drive, must produce daily to feel functional
3. Analytical — data-first, skeptical, requires evidence not inspiration
4. Individualization — keen observer of unique qualities, systems thinker
5. Competition — progress measured by comparison, responds to metrics

**Personality: INTJ (SCOAI variant)**
- 67% Introversion, 50% Intuition, 50% Thinking, 44% Judging
- Self-directed, systems-oriented, high standards, analytical
- Responds poorly to emotional pressure, vague motivation, or visual noise
- Distrusts manipulation — can detect "designed to feel inspiring" immediately
- Currently in a recovery phase (physical + operational reintegration)

**What this profile responds to:**
- Evidence, not encouragement
- Precision, not warmth
- Ecological observation, not moral framing
- Operational language ("system has been quiet") not evaluative ("you're doing great")

**What this profile is especially vulnerable to:**
- Shame spirals from punitive visual signals
- Dopamine-seeking behavior if the interface rewards in satisfying ways
- Goodhart's Law — optimizing the metric instead of the behavior
- Analytical paralysis from visual complexity

## THE APP ARCHITECTURE

Five tabs: **Home, Today, Increments, Habits, You**

**Home:** Morning evidence card + 5-dot system status row + One Door interrupt card (appears when nothing completed by noon)

**Today:** Energy state selector (Full/Partial/Reserve) + action stack (max 8, capped) + Daily Review ritual (end of day, 3 questions)

**Increments:** Five life systems — Environment, Cognition, Health, Operations, Participation — each with score (0-100), 7-day trend, quiet signal

**Habits:** Recurring habits with 7-dot completion trail, cue anchoring, minimum scope

**You:** Operator Profile, phase label (identity marker), notification controls, guardrails

**Milestone moments (non-tab):** Day 14 calibration review, Day 30 pattern surface, Day 66 automaticity audit, Day 90 phase recognition

**The existing icon:**

Dark near-black background. It reads as: precise, anatomical, technical, warm at the center, not illustrative or cartoonish.

**What this icon establishes:**
The brain represents the cognitive system this app supports. The warm gold nodes represent the neural connections that behavioral consistency builds. The violet exterior represents the operational system. The visual language is: anatomical precision + warm internal life.

## THE CORE DESIGN TENSION

The app is a precision instrument. It must:
- Feel inhabitable on low-energy days without becoming inviting in a way that reads as "designed to be inviting"
- Provide enough atmospheric depth to be worth opening — but not enough to be entertaining
- Support identity reconstruction without becoming a self-help product
- Be used daily for 90+ days without visual fatigue

---

## THE QUESTION

The user is considering adding imagery to some screens or tabs. They don't know what kind, what role, or where. They want the science to answer this before any visual decisions are made.

This is the correct instinct. Answer it scientifically.

---

## YOUR TASK

Answer each section in sequence. Be specific. Cite mechanisms. Do not defer to aesthetic preference.

---

### SECTION 1 — DOES THIS APP NEED IMAGERY AT ALL?

1. **The null hypothesis.** What does the research say about the role of imagery in behavioral tools compared to purely typographic/data-driven interfaces? For which user profiles and use cases does imagery add measurable behavioral value — and for which does it reduce it?

2. **The INTJ/Analytical case specifically.** Research on INTJ and high-Analytical profiles and their response to imagery in functional tools. Does this profile benefit from imagery, or does imagery introduce interpretive load that consumes cognitive resources better spent on the behavioral task?

3. **The recovery-phase variable.** The user is in physical and operational recovery. What does the literature on imagery and recovery states say? Does imagery support or undermine behavioral reintegration? Under what conditions does imagery become a crutch (passive consumption replacing active engagement)?

4. **The identity reconstruction variable.** The app is partly a tool for identity consolidation — "who am I becoming" during a difficult transition. What role, if any, does imagery play in identity-supporting interfaces? Does research support visual metaphor as an identity anchor, or does it introduce ambiguity that weakens rather than strengthens self-concept?

5. **The 90-day habituation question.** What happens to imagery in a daily-use interface over 90+ days? Does it decay in effectiveness? Does it create novelty expectations that undermine the "transparent instrument" goal? What does research on long-term imagery exposure in habit tools say?

---

### SECTION 2 — IF IMAGERY IS WARRANTED, WHERE DOES IT BELONG?

*Answer this section only if Section 1 supports the inclusion of imagery. If the science suggests no imagery, skip Section 2 and go directly to Section 3.*

6. **Screen-level analysis.** For each of the five main tabs (Home, Today, Increments, Habits, You) and the milestone moments (Days 14, 30, 66, 90), evaluate independently:
   - Does this screen's function benefit from imagery?
   - What job would the imagery do here (atmosphere, orientation, metaphor, identity anchoring, other)?
   - What is the risk of imagery on this screen?
   - Verdict: warranted / not warranted / conditional

7. **Morning vs. evening context.** The app is used at wake time (low arousal, cortisol rising) and at end-of-day (cognitively depleted). Do these two physiological states have different imagery needs? Should any imagery be time-of-day conditional?

8. **Reserve state.** When the user selects "Reserve" energy state (lowest capacity), what is the research-supported approach to imagery? Should it increase (supportive/comforting), decrease (reduce load), or stay neutral?

---

### SECTION 3 — IF IMAGERY IS WARRANTED, WHAT KIND?

*Answer this section only if Section 1 supports inclusion and Section 2 identifies specific warranted locations.*

9. **The brain icon as visual language anchor.** The existing icon establishes a specific visual vocabulary: anatomical precision, warm gold neural nodes on violet-indigo field, warm corona at center, dark background. What imagery types are semantically consistent with this language? What types would betray it?

10. **Abstract vs. representational.** What does research on abstract imagery vs. representational imagery in behavioral tools say? For an Analytical/INTJ profile, does abstract imagery (geometric, structural, mathematical) or representational imagery (natural environments, human figures, objects) produce better behavioral engagement or worse?

11. **The "nature vs. instrument" question.** Research on nature imagery in digital environments (attention restoration theory) vs. instrument/technical imagery. For a tool explicitly designed as a precision instrument for an Analytical/INTJ, which visual register is more appropriate? Is there a documented cost to mixing these registers?

12. **Imagery and shame.** What does research on imagery in recovery and behavioral contexts say about the risk of aspirational imagery (images of peak performance, ideal states, "what you're working toward")? For a profile vulnerable to shame spirals, does aspirational imagery motivate or punish?

13. **The motion question.** If any imagery is warranted, what does research say about static vs. animated imagery in a daily-use behavioral tool for this profile? Is there a documented threshold beyond which animation becomes stimulation rather than communication?

---

### SECTION 4 — IMPLEMENTATION CONSTRAINTS

*Answer this section if any imagery is warranted.*

14. **Cognitive load budget.** If the app includes imagery, what is the maximum amount before the interface shifts from "instrument with atmospheric depth" to "visually stimulating environment"? How should this budget be allocated across screens?

15. **The established icon's visual language.** Given the specific visual vocabulary of the brain icon (anatomical, warm gold nodes, violet field, dark background), provide specific guidance on what any additional imagery must include or exclude to remain in the same register. Give concrete parameters, not aesthetic guidance.

16. **What not to image.** List specific imagery categories that are contraindicated for this profile in this context, with mechanism. Examples may include: aspirational lifestyle imagery, human figures, bright natural landscapes, abstract art with strong emotional tone, etc. Be specific about why each category fails.

---

### SECTION 5 — THE ALTERNATIVE HYPOTHESIS

17. **Could the atmospheric depth goal be achieved without imagery?** The user wants the app to feel inhabited and worth opening daily. What does research say about non-imagery methods of achieving atmospheric depth — typography, whitespace, motion, depth/parallax, sound, haptics — and which of these are more appropriate for the INTJ/Analytical profile than imagery?

18. **The brand icon as the only image.** Many precision instruments use a single symbolic mark — a logo or emblem — as their only visual representation, with all other content typographic. Evaluate this approach for INCREMENTS. Is the brain icon sufficient as the system's sole image? What would be lost and gained by this constraint?

---

### SECTION 6 — SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS

After completing the analysis above, provide a clear verdict:

**Primary verdict:** Does INCREMENTS need imagery beyond the existing brain icon? Yes / No / Conditional (specify conditions).

If yes or conditional, provide:
- A ranked list of the specific screens/moments where imagery is warranted
- For each: the job the imagery should do, the type that is scientifically appropriate, and the type that would cause harm
- A cognitive load budget (how much imagery total, and how distributed)
- The three most important constraints for any imagery to remain consistent with the behavioral science architecture

If no:
- What the existing visual system should do instead to achieve atmospheric depth
- Why the "no imagery" constraint is actually a design strength for this profile

---

### SECTION 7 — WHAT THE SCIENCE SAYS THE USER ACTUALLY WANTS

This is the most important section.

The user said: "I don't know — I want to focus on what the science says."

This is a signal, not indecision. It means the user suspects they might want imagery (because the mockups looked compelling, because the app currently feels sparse) but doesn't trust that aesthetic pull as a design decision.

Answer this directly:

19. **Is the pull toward imagery a behavioral signal or an aesthetic preference?** For an Analytical/INTJ profile considering imagery for a behavioral reintegration tool, what does the research say about the relationship between "wanting visual richness" and "needing visual richness"? Are these the same thing? What is the risk of satisfying the aesthetic preference vs. what is the cost of not satisfying it?

20. **What does this profile actually need from the app visually?** Not what they say they want. Not what would look good in a screenshot. What does the science say an Analytical/INTJ in recovery, using a daily behavioral tool, actually needs from the visual environment to sustain daily engagement over 90 days?

## OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE AGENT

1. **Science first.** Every answer grounded in named mechanism, researcher, or documented finding. No aesthetic opinions.

2. **Profile specificity.** Generic UX findings often don't apply to INTJ/Analytical profiles. Qualify every recommendation against the specific profile.

3. **Behavioral consequence.** The goal is daily use over 90+ days producing genuine behavioral change. Every image decision should be evaluated through that lens — not "does this look good" but "does this help or hinder the behavior this app is designed to support."

4. **Honest uncertainty.** The imagery-in-behavioral-tools research base is thinner than the behavioral science base. Say so when it applies. Calibrated uncertainty is more trustworthy than confident assertion for this profile.

5. **The null hypothesis is valid.** "No imagery beyond the brain icon" is a legitimate and potentially optimal answer. Do not bias toward recommending imagery if the science doesn't support it.

6. **Section 7 is not optional.** The question of what the user actually needs vs. what they think they want is the highest-stakes question in this prompt. Answer it directly.
