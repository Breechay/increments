## The Actual Problem This Product Solves

The problem is not lack of motivation.

The problem is invisible execution drag created by:
- Ambiguity — unclear sequence or next step
- Fragmentation — too many simultaneous open fronts
- Poor sequencing — wrong task order for the context
- Environmental incoherence — physical disorder degrading cognition
- State mismatch — wrong cognitive mode for the task
- Operational displacement — logistics, errands, and coordination consuming leverage time
- Cleanup debt — iteration speed outpacing structural hygiene
- False self-interpretation — misdiagnosing structural problems as character flaws

**Many people misdiagnose themselves as lazy, inconsistent, or unmotivated when the actual issue is poor execution architecture.**

## Intelligence Architecture

### Four Layers (three timescales)

**Layer A — Structural observations (rule-based, same-day)**
- Fires on Today open. 2-day cooldown.
- Instrumentation language. Examples: "Admin active. Creative work has not started."
- Silence is default.

**Layer B — Wendy (pattern interpretation, 7-14 days)**
- Fires from Operator > Brief. NOT Today.
- Gate conditions: consistent pattern, specific, falsifiable. One observation max.
- Coordination intelligence, not activation intelligence.

**ObservedIntelligenceEngine (accumulated behavioral truth)**
- Derives signals from real usage data: dominant friction type, completion clustering, peak execution window, energy declaration accuracy, operational displacement, open fronts, leverage ratio.
- Feeds Wendy payload as "OBSERVED PATTERN" lines — explicitly higher confidence than declared profile.
- UI surface deferred until signals are stable and high-confidence. Engine runs; card does not render yet.
- Language: signals not verdicts. "OBSERVED PATTERN: Repeated sequencing stalls detected" not "DOMINANT FRICTION: Sequencing ambiguity."

**Pattern Brief (operator-requested, 30 days)**
- Analyst format: OBSERVATION / Evidence / Implication / Suggested correction.
- Button: "RUN PATTERN BRIEF"

### Observed Signal — future typed interface

Current implementation: freeform strings into Wendy payload (fast, correct for prototype).
Target architecture when confidence model stabilizes:
```
ObservedSignal { type, confidence, evidenceWindowDays, message, uiLabel }
confidence: low(silence) / medium(tentative) / high(direct) / confirmed(intervention)
uiLabel: nil = don't surface yet
```

Replace `wendyPayloadLines: [String]` → `[ObservedSignal]` when ready.

### Buy-In Mechanics (operator-specific)

Adherence is highest when:
- The mechanism is understood (why this timing, why this sequence, why this not that)
- The consequence of deviation is visible (not abstract)
- The action has a clear role in a larger system he has bought into

Adherence breaks when:
- Instructions feel arbitrary (no reason given)
- The sequence is unclear
- The action feels disconnected from outcome

This is the operational reason notes in INCREMENTS contain mechanism, not just instruction. It is not verbosity. It is adherence infrastructure.

CORRECT REGISTER: Briefing. Diagnostic. Precise. Coordination-focused. High-agency.

## Explanation Doctrine

Explanation is not decorative. Correct explanation reduces resistance, improves compliance, frees cognitive bandwidth, and improves self-accuracy. But explanation must be causal, specific, earned, and behavior-linked — not generic educational filler.

**Core operator truth (observed May 2026):**
> "The science behind that makes me want to do them more and actually look forward to it."

For this operator, adherence does not improve primarily through guilt, streak pressure, motivational language, or simplification. It improves through **causal understanding + structural clarity**. This is a materially different behavioral model.

When mechanism materially improves adherence or execution quality, explanation is not verbosity — it is infrastructure.

**Two-layer architecture for action notes:**

Layer 1 — Execution cue (always present, 1–2 lines)
Immediate, specific, no friction. Example: "Apply to damp skin within 3 min of shower. Feet + elbows extra."

Layer 2 — Mechanism (present where it changes execution quality)
The causal reason. Example: "Lotion seals existing water; dry skin absorbs far less. Damp skin within 3 min = materially better absorption."

**Explain when:**
- Timing-dependent interventions (collagen pre-lift, niacinamide wait time, minoxidil dry window)
- Sequencing-sensitive protocols (floss before brush, niacinamide before eye cream)
- Irreversible mistakes (no fabric softener on athletic wear, brushing immediately after acidic drinks)
- Invisible long-term consequences (UVA aging without burning, fluoride rinsed away by water)
- Causal mechanisms that increase trust and remove arbitrary compliance

**Do not explain:**
- Self-evident mechanical tasks ("take out trash")
- Actions where explanation adds friction without changing execution
- Anything where glance → essay → fatigue is the result

**Target interaction:** glance → understand → execute

**Implication for Wendy:**
Intelligence interventions should use mechanism, not nagging.
Wrong: "You've skipped PM oral care 3 times."
Right: "Skipping floss before brushing reduces bacterial clearance — brushing after flossing is what makes it effective."
That is calibrated intervention, not pressure.

**Implication for product:** When explanation materially improves adherence, explanation is infrastructure. Optimize for intelligent sense — not ease.

## Instruction Quality Doctrine

Distinct from Explanation Doctrine.

**Explanation = why. Instruction = how.**

These are separate failure modes. An action can have good explanation (mechanism understood) but bad instruction (technique wrong). Both erode trust — but at different times. Bad explanation causes resistance before execution. Bad instruction causes disappointment after execution.

Execution actions that have meaningful technique differences should include correct operational instruction — not just mechanism, but correct procedure.

**Examples where instruction quality materially changes outcome:**
- Lotion timing (damp skin within 3 min, not after drying)
- Scalp massage technique (tension not friction)
- SPF dosing (two fingers, not a thin coat)
- Warmup mechanics (movement pattern specificity)
- Oral hygiene order (floss → brush → scrape, no rinse)
- Laundry protocol (no fabric softener on athletic wear, cold water)
- Minoxidil timing (dry scalp, not damp)

**Discovering a preventable technique error after the fact degrades trust.** The system recommended it; the system should have taught it correctly.

Correct instruction increases both outcomes and adherence. It is not hand-holding — it is precision.

## Constraint Taxonomy (Reserve vs Compressed)

These are not equivalent operator states.

**RESERVE:**
Internal constraint. The organism is the limiting factor.
- Energy depleted
- Body signaling recovery need
- Sleep deficit, illness, overtraining
- Correct response: Infrastructure only. Protect the organism. No high-cognitive load.

**COMPRESSED:**
External constraint. The schedule is the limiting factor.
- Time window disrupted
- Calendar pressure, unexpected obligation
- Organism is capable but window is reduced
- Correct response: Continuity + phase survival. Protect the thread. Do not abandon.

**Reserve = protect the organism.**
**Compressed = protect the continuity.**

Misrouting these is a real failure mode. RESERVE logic applied to a COMPRESSED day abandons productive work unnecessarily. COMPRESSED logic applied to a RESERVE day overrides recovery need.

## Mechanism Confidence Levels

Not all mechanisms behind INCREMENTS actions carry equal evidential weight. Internal metadata should reflect this — to prevent Wendy from overstating uncertain science and to preserve trust.

**Confidence levels:**

**Established** — Replicated, mechanistically understood, high consensus.
Examples: creatine saturation kinetics · zinc/copper antagonism · SPF dosing physics · melatonin suppression timing · fluoride rinsing behavior

**Strong** — Well-supported, broadly accepted, mechanistically plausible.
Examples: collagen pre-loading window · oral hygiene sequence · athletic fabric detergent sensitivity · hydration-output correlation

**Emerging** — Early evidence, plausible mechanism, not yet replicated at scale.
Examples: scalp massage hair density pathways · some cold exposure mood protocols · certain recovery sequencing claims

**Model (when implemented):**
```swift
enum MechanismConfidence {
    case established
    case strong
    case emerging
}
```

**Doctrine:** Wendy should not present emerging mechanisms with established confidence. If the science is preliminary, the mechanism note should reflect that — or be omitted. Trust is the product. Overstating evidence destroys it faster than silence.
